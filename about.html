Practice

My work sits at the intersection of design, research, and cultural observation. I engage with spaces, systems, and narratives through a hands-on approach—whether through fieldwork, design interventions, or collaborative projects.

I believe in making as a form of thinking, and in research as a creative practice. The projects I undertake are explorations of how we inhabit, understand, and transform our environments.

Interest

I am drawn to the overlooked, the vernacular, and the in-between. Rural landscapes, cultural systems, and the everyday rituals that shape collective life—these are the subjects that animate my curiosity.

I'm interested in how traditions adapt, how communities evolve, and how design can serve as a tool for both documentation and intervention. My research explores the relationship between place, identity, and memory.

Direction

Moving forward, I aim to deepen my practice at the intersection of empirical research and conceptual exploration. I want to develop methodologies that bridge observation and speculation, grounding theory in lived experience while imagining alternative futures.

I see my work evolving toward collaborative, long-term engagements with communities and places—projects that grow organically, respond to context, and contribute to ongoing conversations about culture, sustainability, and social transformation.
